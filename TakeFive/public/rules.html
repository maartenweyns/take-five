Game Rules

brightness_4

Take Five, also known as 6 Nimmt, is a card game that's fast and fun to play! Let's quickly go over how to play it exactly!

Take Five starts with each player receiving 10 cards, and four cards being put on the table, set up in 4 rows. The goal of the game is to complete the four started rows with your cards, gathering as little penalty points as possible. Every card has at least one penalty point, but some have more. The amount of penalty points a card is worth is indicated by the number of cow heads on the card. Cards with different amounts of penalty points are also different in color.

In every turn, each player chooses a card from their hand. The choice of this card can be confirmed by clicking the confirm button. When all players chose a card, the chosen cards are appended to the rows. The card with the lowest value will be appended first, followed by all other cards with increasing values. The cards are appended to the rows obeying the following rules:

  • The row must go in increasing order
  • A card should be appended to the row where it has the least difference with the last card of that row
  • visibilityExample 1

    If a player were to play the card 53, the card should go on the second row.

If a player plays the sixth card of a certain row, the player is obligated to take the first five cards of that row (take five 😉) and their card will become the first card of the row. This player has now collected penalty points, the amount of which being the sum of all the penalty points of the cards the player just took. Every ten rounds, the penalty points are subtracted from the player's score.

If a player plays a card that does not fit any row (i.e. the card has a lower value than all the last cards of the rows), the player can pick a row. This row will be taken by the player (as penalty points) and the player's card will become the first card of this row.

The game is played until one player has survived, that is until all other players have a score below zero. If multiple players die in the last round, the player with the highest remaining score wins.
